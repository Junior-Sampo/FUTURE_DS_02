Customer Churn Dashboard – Key Insights & Business Recommendations

This project analyzes customer churn behavior using subscription data, focusing on retention trends, customer segments, and key drivers of churn.

Key Insights:
   . Contract type is the strongest churn driver: Customers on month-to-month contracts show significantly higher churn compared to one- and two-year contracts.
   . Customer tenure impacts retention: Churn is highest among new customers (0–12 months) and declines as tenure increases, indicating that long-term customers are more stable.
   . High-value customers are at risk: Customers with higher monthly charges contribute more to revenue but also exhibit notable churn levels.
   . Service-related factors matter: Customers using fiber optic internet and those without additional support services (e.g., Tech Support) tend to churn more frequently.

Business Recommendations:
   . Improve onboarding and early engagement, especially within the first 12 months, where churn is highest.
   . Bundle value-added services (e.g., Tech Support, Online Security) to increase customer stickiness.
   . Encourage long-term contracts through discounts or incentives to reduce churn risk.

Business Value:
By identifying high-risk customer segments and key churn drivers, this analysis enables businesses to:
   . Improve customer retention strategies
   . Make data-driven decisions that directly impact revenue and growth
